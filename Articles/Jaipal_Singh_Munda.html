Jaipal Singh Munda

An exceptional man with extraordinary career who emerged as a politician,profolic writer and a sportsperson. Jaipal Singh Munda, born on 3 January 1903 in a tribal family, belonged to the state which having resources worth 15000 crores i.e.Jharkhand. His real name was Pramod Pahan,famous as "the proud jangli". He was a child who used to look after cattle herd, and after schooling at village church he moved on to St.Paul's College at Ranchi in 1910, and it proved to be a turning point of his life. He was an excellent with exceptional qualities from young age. His principal,Canon Crosgrave took his wings. He accompanied his principal when she returned to England in 1918. And in 1922 he enrolled at University of Oxford, at St.John's College. Here,he had shown outstanding performances in academics as well as sports. He became a university team member in hockey. He chose Economics as his subject and graduated with honors in 1926. After his graduation, he undertook the ICS exam, in which he was selected and required two years of oxford training.

Luckily after few years he got selected in Indian Hockey Team, in which he had to compete at Amsterdam Summer Olympics in 1928. His team included one of his senior Nawab of Pataudi and famous Dhyan Chand . Due to the excellent game of Jaipal Singh, his team easily won all the matches but unfortunately, he could not play the finals as he had to return to London back due to his ICS final exams. His team made history by winning the first-ever Olympic gold by beating Holland with 3-0. The hallmarks of his game as a defender were excellent tackling, sensible gameplay and well-directed hard hits. He too was the most versatile player in Oxford University hockey team. His contribution to the university hockey team was recognized and he became the first Indian student to be conferred blue in hockey. He becamehumilated because despite of scoring highest marks in ICS exam, he was told to repeat a year as he has broken the rule by joining the Olympics, so due to this he refused and ended his professional career. He then took up job as senior executive. Then he moved to Ghana and within the other three years, he returned to India. In 1937, he took a job as a principal at Rajkumar College in Raipur. But due to the outrage of the prince, Jaipal Singh was forced to resign. After moving here and there for years, he finally decided to return to his hometown, Ranchi. There,he helped many tribal for their upliftment. In 1946, the first-ever Adivasi was appointed to the constituent Assembly, to represent the tribal population. His once even quoted..

"What my people require, sir is not adequate safeguards...They require protection from the ministers. We do not ask for special protection. We want to be treated to be like other Indians."

He also had worked a lot in the sphere of education. He wrote many letters to Dr.Rajnendra Prasad, asking him to help in Bihar Education sector,but unfortunately his letter got denied. So, he continued his works for the upliftment of tribal. Jaipal Singh Munda Continued to work for his tribal till his last breath in 1970. It was very sad for him when he saw his dream of equality not coming true for tribes as they remain a work of progress.

As the sun sets down today but will rise tomorrow, similarly although Jaipal Singh may today be alive but we have faith that there would be a likeliness of his in future.....
